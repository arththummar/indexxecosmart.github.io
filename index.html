Where do we get Energy from?

Due to the dependence of human life and daily functions on energy, humans have found ways to mass produce and create energy.
The top three ways that humans have learned to create energy include the burning of fossil fuels, and are nonrenewable.

1. Petroleum: 36%

  • Petroleum is the highest source of energy in the United States
  • In the United States, petroleum is most abundantly found in Texas, California and Alaska.
  • Historically the most consumed energy source in terms of annual U.S energy consumption
  • In 2021, America made up 20.1% of all petroleum usage in the world, the highest of all nations.

2. Natural Gas: 33%

  • Primarily used for heat generation as well as electricity.
  • In 2022, oil contributed 353 million metric tons of CO2 in US.
  • This can have very poor effects on the environment as more pollutants are in the air. This can lead to climate change or even human diseases.

3. Renewable Energy: 13%

  • Among the renewable energy sources, Biomass energy sources are used the most (39% of renewable energy)
  • Renewable energy uses materials that are constantly available and will not run out, which avoids risk of resource depletion
  • The government is incentivizing citizens to implement renewable energy sources in their own lives through tax rebates!
